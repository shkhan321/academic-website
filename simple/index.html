About

Dr. Sanan H. Khan is an Associate Professor of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at United Arab Emirates University. He completed his Ph.D. in Solid Mechanics and Design at IIT Kanpur in 2018, followed by postdoctoral research at IIT Delhi and a faculty post at Aligarh Muslim University before joining UAEU. His work sits where experiment meets simulation: he builds and tests composite and fiber-metal structures in the lab, then reproduces their behaviour with finite-element models. Recent projects span impact and ballistic damage in layered materials, shape-memory-alloy actuators for adaptive wings, 3D-printed and multi-material components, and the durability of Type-IV hydrogen storage vessels — much of it carried out alongside his graduate students.

Research Interests

  • Composite and fiber-metal laminate design, testing, and failure analysis
  • Impact, damage, and ballistic protection of layered materials
  • Finite-element and progressive-damage modelling (Abaqus)
  • Additive manufacturing and multi-material components
  • Smart structures and shape-memory-alloy actuation
  • AI / ML for engineering design and optimisation

Laboratory

My lab supports both experimental and computational mechanics: an Abaqus finite-element workstation, 2D digital image correlation for full-field strain measurement, hand lay-up and surface-treatment facilities for fiber-metal laminates, surface metrology, FDM 3D printing, and a laser engraver. Equipment is available for collaborative and student research — please contact me to arrange access.
