Research of the Month · June 2026

Stacking-sequence effects in asymmetric CFRP laminates

How should a carbon-fibre laminate be stacked so that it bends predictably and fails gracefully? This month's highlight pairs physical three-point bending tests with ply-level finite-element models to reveal how the stacking sequence of asymmetric CFRP laminates governs their flexural stiffness, the progression of damage, and the energy absorbed before failure — insight that feeds directly into lighter, tougher aerospace structures.

Current Research

My current research is centered on original experimental and computational studies in aerospace materials, smart structures, and aerodynamic optimization. The work combines impact and progressive damage modeling of layered composite and fiber-metal structures, flexural failure analysis of asymmetric CFRP laminates, shape-memory-alloy actuation for smart aircraft wings, and CFD/AI-based optimization of UAV airfoils.
