Biography

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at United Arab Emirates University (UAEU). I received my Ph.D. in Solid Mechanics and Design from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kanpur in 2018, followed by postdoctoral research at IIT Delhi and a faculty appointment at Aligarh Muslim University before joining UAEU.

My work sits at the interface of experiment and simulation — I build and test composite and fiber-metal laminate structures in the laboratory and model their behaviour with finite-element analysis. My research interests include impact and progressive damage in layered materials, ballistic protection, shape-memory-alloy actuation for adaptive aircraft wings, additive and multi-material manufacturing, and the durability of Type-IV hydrogen storage vessels, increasingly supported by AI/ML methods for design and optimisation.

I have authored over 50 peer-reviewed journal and conference papers (h-index 16) and lead several funded projects as Principal Investigator, together exceeding AED 1.2 million, with partners including Boeing, Al Ain Municipality, and UAEU. I teach undergraduate and graduate courses in mechanics, finite-element methods, and aerospace structures, and I supervise BSc, MSc, and PhD students in my research group.
